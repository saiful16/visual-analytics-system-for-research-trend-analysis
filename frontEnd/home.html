This application is a multi-level visual analytics system designed to help users explore research trends within the domain of computer science.

Aim: To enable users to explore broad research fields, drill down into specific concepts, and identify emerging or dominant trends through structured visual analysis.

A Guide for Users:

Use the following levels to systematically analyze research publications, start with broad fields, then refine your focus through subfields, topics, and concepts to identify and validate emerging trends.

Level 1: Field Overview

  • What it shows: Overall publication trends across different major fields.
  • How to use it: Observe long-term trends; identify fields with consistent or rapid growth.
  • Goal: Identify which major research fields are trending.

Level 2: Subfield Overview

  • What it shows: Growth rates, publication sizes, and topic makeup within a field.
  • How to use it: Drill down into subfields to find fast-growing or high-volume areas.
  • Goal: Pinpoint trending subfields within the broader domain.

Level 3: Topic Details

  • What it shows: Trends and comparisons of a selected topic
  • How to use it: Identify specific topics with high or rising interest over time.
  • Goal: Discover the trend of a selected topic.

Level 4: Concept Detail

  • What it shows: Concept-level insights via word clouds, timelines, and heatmaps.
  • How to use it: View high-frequency or newly emerging concepts under a topic.
  • Goal: Recognize trending or influential concepts in research.

Validation Strategy

Each level includes follow-up visualizations to validate identified trends:

  • Use size and growth metrics to confirm trend intensity.
  • Use comparisons to evaluate relative performance.
  • Use concept views to discover new or popular research ideas.

Key Metrics Explained

These metrics support trend identification and comparison at each level:

  • Year: The publication year used for time-based analysis.
  • Publication Count: Total number of publications for a given entity.
  • Growth Rate: Annual percentage increase or decrease in publication volume.
  • Subfield Size: Total publication volume of a subfield over time.
  • Subfield Average: Average metric (e.g., growth or count) for a subfield compared internally.
  • Global Average: Benchmark metrics averaged across all subfields/topics for comparison.
  • Top Concepts: Frequently occurring or highly weighted terms within a topic.
  • Concept Level: Hierarchical layer indicating the depth or abstraction level of a concept (e.g., general vs. specific).
